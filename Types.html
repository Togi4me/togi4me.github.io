Different Types of Guinea Pigs

Since there are so many different breeds of guinea pigs to get, this page will show the different types of pigs, and what they look like.

Rex
Rex pigs are notable
for their short, spiky
hair and curly whiskers.
My own Rex, Will, is very
energetic and attention
seeking.
Abyssinian
Abyssinian piggies have s-
piky, long fur and regular,
straight whiskers.
Lola is an
Abyssinian, and is very
much a drama queen.
American
American guinea pigs
have short, flat hair
and regular, straight
whiskers
Charlotte is an American
pig, and is quite shy, but
is friendly to family.
Teddy
Teddy pigs, like the Rex breed,
have short, spiky hair, that's
sometimes curly.
Their whiskers are also
straight.
Skinny
Skinny guinea pigs have alm-
ost no hair on their body,
only a little on their face.
The pigs still have color
patches on their body,
showing what color the
fur would be if it was
there. Skinny pigs have
short, curly whiskers.
Peruvian
Pervuian pigs have LONG
hair all over their body.
These pigs do need to get
haircuts periodically so
that their hair doesn't
become too messy. Whiskers
are normal.
Sheltie
Sheltie pigs have long
hair as well, but not
quite as Peruvians.
Shelties need haircuts,
and have regular
whiskers.
Texel
Texel piggies have curly,
long hair all over thier
body. These pigs will
need haircuts periodically
as well. Whiskers are VERY
curly.
Himalayan
Himilayan pigs are
short haired and have
regular whiskers, just
like the American breed.
The main difference is
that Himalayan pigs have
black noses, ears, and paws
red eyes, and white
bodies.
Cuy
Cuy pigs are unusually
large, American breed
guinea pigs. Since these
pigs are so large, they
will need more food and
water than the average
little ones.
